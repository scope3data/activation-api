**Synthetic Audiences** define your target audience profiles, demographics, and behavioral characteristics for advertising campaigns. These audiences use natural language prompts to articulate your ideal customer profiles, informing campaign targeting, creative development, and messaging strategies.

## Overview

Synthetic Audiences provide:
- **Target audience definition** and behavioral profiling
- **Campaign targeting guidance** for precise audience reach across all campaigns
- **Audience insight generation** based on your customer profile definitions
- **Multi-market adaptation** with country, channel, and language specificity

## Core Properties

<ResponseField name="id" type="string" required>
  Unique identifier for the synthetic audience
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="name" type="string" required>
  Human-readable name for the audience (e.g., "Tech Enthusiasts", "Luxury Shoppers")
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="brands" type="string[]" required>
  Array of brand names this audience applies to. Empty array means all brands within the brand agent
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="channels" type="string[]" required>
  Array of channel codes this audience targets (e.g., ["web", "social", "mobile"]). Empty array means all channels
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="countries" type="string[]" required>
  Array of ISO country codes this audience targets (e.g., ["US", "CA", "GB"]). Empty array means all countries
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="languages" type="string[]" required>
  Array of language codes this audience targets (e.g., ["en", "es", "fr"]). Required for storage - cannot be empty
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="prompt" type="string" required>
  Natural language prompt defining your target audience profile, demographics, and behavioral characteristics
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="createdAt" type="datetime" required>
  ISO 8601 timestamp when the audience was created
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="updatedAt" type="datetime" required>
  ISO 8601 timestamp when the audience was last modified
</ResponseField>

## Common Use Cases

### Premium Lifestyle Audience
Define affluent professionals who value quality and authentic experiences.

```
"Target affluent professionals aged 25-45 with household incomes above $100K. They value craftsmanship, sustainability, and authentic experiences. They prioritize quality over quantity, prefer timeless design, and practice conscious consumption. They respond to confident but approachable messaging and appreciate expertise without pretension."
```

### Enterprise Technology Audience
Define decision-makers in business technology adoption.

```
"Target CTOs, tech directors, and innovation leaders at mid-to-large enterprises (500+ employees). They're responsible for technology strategy and vendor selection. They value practical outcomes, measurable results, and proven ROI. They prefer authoritative yet accessible communication and are influenced by industry thought leadership and peer recommendations."
```

### Community-Focused Audience
Define community-minded consumers who support local businesses.

```
"Target community-minded individuals aged 30-55 who prioritize supporting local economy and small businesses. They value authenticity, personal relationships, and community impact. They're more likely to make purchase decisions based on local recommendations and personal connections. They respond to warm, personal messaging and are influenced by real community stories and testimonials."
```

## Audience Applications

### Campaign Targeting
Synthetic audiences directly influence campaign targeting:
- **Targeting Parameters**: Demographics, interests, and behavioral signals
- **Audience Definition**: Precise customer characteristics and motivations  
- **Channel Strategy**: Appropriate channels based on audience preferences
- **Content Relevance**: Topics and messaging that resonate with target audiences

### Audience Segmentation
Audiences define precise customer segments:
- **Demographics**: Age, income, education levels and geographic location
- **Psychographics**: Values, interests, lifestyle preferences and motivations
- **Behavioral Patterns**: Purchase behavior, decision-making factors, and engagement preferences
- **Channel Preferences**: Where target audiences consume content and make decisions

### Creative Targeting
Audience profiles guide creative development:
- **Message Resonance**: Content that appeals to specific audience characteristics
- **Visual Preferences**: Imagery and design choices that align with audience tastes
- **Content Themes**: Topics and messages that engage the target demographic
- **Call-to-Action Strategy**: Actions that align with audience behavior patterns and preferences

## Best Practices

### Audience Development
- **Be specific**: Audiences should include detailed demographic and behavioral characteristics
- **Know your customers**: Include specific customer insights and preferences
- **Stay focused**: Ensure all audience elements work together to define a coherent segment
- **Consider journey stages**: Address different stages of customer awareness and purchase behavior

### Prompt Writing
- **Include demographic details**: Specific age ranges, income levels, and geographic information
- **Define behavioral patterns**: Clear guidance on how the audience makes decisions
- **Specify preferences**: What this audience values, their interests, and motivations
- **Provide context**: Industry knowledge, competitive preferences, purchase drivers

### Targeting Strategy
- **Market-specific audiences**: Create different profiles for different regions or segments
- **Channel adaptation**: Adjust audience characteristics for different media channels
- **Language localization**: Ensure cultural relevance and appropriate targeting in different markets
- **Evolution capability**: Build audiences that can evolve with changing market conditions and customer behavior
