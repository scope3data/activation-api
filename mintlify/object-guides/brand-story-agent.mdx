**Brand Stories** define your brand narrative, messaging strategy, and audience engagement approach for advertising campaigns. These stories use natural language prompts to articulate your brand's voice and positioning, informing campaign strategies, audience targeting, and creative development.

## Overview

Brand Stories provide:
- **Brand narrative definition** and messaging strategy
- **Campaign strategy guidance** for consistent brand voice across all campaigns
- **Audience insight generation** based on your brand story elements
- **Multi-market adaptation** with country, channel, and language specificity

## Core Properties

<ResponseField name="id" type="string" required>
  Unique identifier for the brand story
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="name" type="string" required>
  Human-readable name for the story (e.g., "Premium Lifestyle Story", "Tech Innovation Narrative")
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="brands" type="string[]" required>
  Array of brand names this story applies to. Empty array means all brands within the brand agent
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="channels" type="string[]" required>
  Array of channel codes this story targets (e.g., ["web", "social", "mobile"]). Empty array means all channels
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="countries" type="string[]" required>
  Array of ISO country codes this story targets (e.g., ["US", "CA", "GB"]). Empty array means all countries
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="languages" type="string[]" required>
  Array of language codes this story targets (e.g., ["en", "es", "fr"]). Required for storage - cannot be empty
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="prompt" type="string" required>
  Natural language prompt defining your brand story, messaging strategy, and audience insights
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="createdAt" type="datetime" required>
  ISO 8601 timestamp when the story was created
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="updatedAt" type="datetime" required>
  ISO 8601 timestamp when the story was last modified
</ResponseField>

## Common Use Cases

### Lifestyle Brand Narrative
Define aspirational messaging for affluent professionals who value quality and authentic experiences.

```
"Our brand represents aspirational living and premium quality. We target affluent professionals aged 25-45 who value craftsmanship, sustainability, and authentic experiences. Our messaging emphasizes quality over quantity, timeless design, and conscious consumption. We speak with confidence but humility, showcasing expertise while remaining approachable."
```

### Tech Innovation Story
Position your brand as a thought leader in business technology solutions.

```
"We're pioneering the future of technology with AI-driven solutions that empower businesses. Our audience includes CTOs, tech directors, and innovation leaders at mid-to-large enterprises. We position ourselves as thought leaders who simplify complex technology. Our tone is authoritative yet accessible, focusing on practical outcomes and measurable results."
```

### Local Community Focus
Emphasize community connection and support for local businesses.

```
"We're deeply rooted in local communities, supporting small businesses and family values. Our customers are community-minded individuals who prioritize supporting local economy. We emphasize authenticity, personal relationships, and community impact. Our messaging is warm, personal, and highlights real community stories and testimonials."
```

## Story Applications

### Campaign Strategy
Brand stories directly influence campaign development:
- **Messaging Framework**: Core themes and value propositions
- **Audience Definition**: Target customer characteristics and motivations  
- **Channel Strategy**: Appropriate channels based on audience preferences
- **Content Themes**: Topics and narratives that resonate with target audiences

### Audience Development
Stories inform synthetic audience creation:
- **Demographics**: Age, income, education levels derived from story
- **Psychographics**: Values, interests, lifestyle preferences
- **Behavioral Patterns**: Purchase behavior and decision-making factors
- **Channel Preferences**: Where target audiences consume content

### Creative Direction
Brand narratives guide creative development:
- **Tone of Voice**: How the brand communicates (professional, friendly, authoritative)
- **Visual Style**: Imagery and design choices that align with brand story
- **Content Themes**: Topics and messages that reinforce brand narrative
- **Call-to-Action Strategy**: Actions that align with customer journey and brand values

## Best Practices

### Story Development
- **Be authentic**: Stories should reflect genuine brand values and positioning
- **Know your audience**: Include detailed audience insights in your narrative
- **Stay consistent**: Ensure all story elements work together coherently
- **Consider journey stages**: Address different stages of customer awareness and consideration

### Prompt Writing
- **Include audience details**: Specific demographic and psychographic information
- **Define tone and voice**: Clear guidance on how the brand communicates
- **Specify differentiators**: What makes this brand unique in the market
- **Provide context**: Industry position, competitive landscape, brand heritage

### Targeting Strategy
- **Market-specific stories**: Create different narratives for different regions or segments
- **Channel adaptation**: Adjust story elements for different media channels
- **Language localization**: Ensure cultural relevance in different markets
- **Evolution capability**: Build stories that can evolve with changing market conditions

### Content Strategy
